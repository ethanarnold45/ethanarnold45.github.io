A Relatively Stand-Alone Proof
of The Whitney-Graustein Theorem

In 1937, Hassler Whitney, with the help of William Graustein, proved two regular closed curves are regular homotopic if and only if they have the same rotation number. Their proof is remarkably beautiful, however, it is dated and is not keeping with modern terminology and definitions in differential topology. Whitney's proof also largely refers to other sources, and he sometimes relies on intuitive steps which he does not rigourously prove.

In this short paper, which will eventually form part of my bachelor's dissertation, I have updated Whitney's proof to match modern language, consolidated his relevent citations, and added more rigour to his original proof.
